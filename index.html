About Me

I've been writing software for quite a while (almost 12 years, yikes!) and I love the process of taking an idea and making it a reality through technology. I believe quality software has the ability to change the world and how we interact with it and I'm always looking for ways I can use my skills in software engineering to improve things.

Writing software is great, but writing quality software gives me the warm fuzzies. I'm a huge proponent of test-driven development, so much so that in 2017 I founded a company specializing in teaching and certifying organizations on the benefits of test-driven development. You can check out our website here. We're really excited about transforming test-driven development into something that all developers can (and want to) do!

Consulting was definitely a great experience, but I got a bit tired of feeling like a second-class citizen (you're on the team, but you aren't really a team member) so I moved on.

The last year has been a learning whirlwind (technologically as I got up to speed on Angular 2+ and also business-wise as I've learned about everything that goes in to securing devices on an enterprise level) and I'm excited to deliver the beta version in the next few months.
Education

In 2015 I earned a Master of Science in Engineering in Engineering Sciences with a Concentration in Software Engineering from Arizona State University (I love saying the full name of the degree). Although the program was challenging I felt like it really enhanced my understanding of the reasons for doing what I'd been doing for 10 years. It was nice to have the why to go with the what.

I completed by Bachelor of Science in Computer Information Systems from DeVry University (yes, DeVry) in 2005. It was a good starting point for what has become a really fun career. In school I got to experience a little bit of everything (hardware, software, networking) and found that I really like solving problems through technology. Specifically, I like being able to change what computers do to make people happy. That education laid the groundwork for everything I've done professionally since.
