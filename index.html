Education
In 2015 I earned a Master of Science in Engineering in Engineering Sciences with a Concentration in Software Engineering from Arizona State University (I love saying the full name of the degree). Although the program was challenging I felt like it really enhanced my understanding of the reasons for doing what I'd been doing for 10 years. It was nice to have the why to go with the what.

I completed by Bachelor of Science in Computer Information Systems from DeVry University (yes, DeVry) in 2005. It was a good starting point for what has become a really fun career. In school I got to experience a little bit of everything (hardware, software, networking) and found that I really like solving problems through technology. Specifically, I like being able to change what computers do to make people happy. That education laid the groundwork for everything I've done professionally since.
Experience
I started my career working for a small order fulfillment company where I got to work on cutting edge technology like dbase (in 2005). While I was there I built a full inventory management system using *drumroll* Microsoft Access! It was pretty impressive, but looking back on it is a bit embarrassing. On the other hand, it was a huge step-up from dbase and Visual FoxPro so I guess that's something.

From there I moved to the exciting world of bulk emails (not spam). I actually learned a huge amount at my second job about SQL Server. It's also where I wrote my first web app (it was really just a web page that saved data to SQL Server, but I'm gonna count it). Those first two jobs really opened my eyes to the fact that I had no idea what I was doing and it was up to me to improve my skills.

After a brief and unfortunate tenure with a small company that didn't end well I moved on to the wonderful world of software consulting. This is where it really, really hit me that I didn't know much about software and I needed to improve my skills significantly and dramatically. Fortunately, I was paired up with a great mentor and the company offered tuition reimbursement so I was able to get that Master's degree up there. Consulting was definitely a great experience, but I got a bit tired of feeling like a second-class citizen (you're on the team, but you aren't really a team member) so I moved on.

Right now I'm very happy with what I'm doing and the team I get to work with everyday. I'm stoked to be working on a great greenfield application that will take a bunch of lessons learned and turn them into a fantastic product.
